Health spending in Malaysia does not flow to where death rates are highest. Six states carry above-average mortality with below-average total health investment — Perak (8.9/1,000), Kedah (8.2), Kelantan, Terengganu, Pahang and Sarawak. Private hospitals concentrate in cities where incomes are already higher, and national averages hide a map where the wheel that squeaks loudest continues to get the grease. "When the money doesn't reach you, you look for a doctor. But in half of Malaysia, there isn't one close enough."

90%

Of Malaysians live in states below the national average of 23 doctors per 10,000. The average hides a deeply unequal map.

East Malaysia bears the sharpest end of this inequality. Sabah and Sarawak — together larger than Peninsular Malaysia — share just 8,198 doctors for 5.9 million people. And these are all doctors: the private-sector specialists in Kota Kinabalu do not serve the interior. For communities in Keningau or Kapit, the effective ratio is far worse than any statistic can capture. "Distance from a doctor kills slowly. But some Malaysians are dying of something faster — diseases we said we'd beaten decades ago."

Across ASEAN, tuberculosis tracks poverty with near-mechanical precision. Countries with poverty rates above 15% carry TB burdens 4–6× higher than their wealthier neighbours. Malaysia's national average looks reassuring — but inside that average, Sabah (19.7% poverty), Kelantan (13.2%), and Sarawak (10.8%) sit in the same poverty tier as Lao PDR and the Philippines. History has a long memory: TB was supposed to be eradicated, but it retreated into the same communities that were always left behind. "Poverty breeds disease. But disease also breeds poverty — especially when a single hospital visit can ruin a family."

Chapter 5 — The Bill That Breaks Families

"One bad hand can clean out the whole table."

B40 households spend 6× more of their income on out-of-pocket healthcare than T20 — 3.6% vs 0.6% in 2023. Every year. Without exception. Source: World Bank + DOSM HIES.

3.7×

Growth in national OOP health spending per capita since 2000 — from $145 to $535 PPP. A post-2016 surge in private-sector costs is pushing the burden higher for everyone, but the B40 feel it most.

For B40 households, out-of-pocket healthcare consumes 3.6% of annual income on average — but averages lie. A single private hospitalisation (RM 5,000–15,000) wipes out 2–5 months of B40 wages. Since 2016, private healthcare costs have surged, adding 0.32 percentage points to the B40's burden alone. The cruelty: the sicker you are, the poorer you get; the poorer you get, the sicker you become. One bad hand cleans out the whole table. "Money protects the rich. But it can't buy clean water and safe food for communities that don't have them."

Chapter 6 — The Infrastructure of Illness

"Rot starts from the roots."

Malaysia's national stunting rate (World Bank, 2022) — almost matching Cambodia's 21.9%, a country with one-fifth the income.

11.4 pp

Malaysia exceeds its income-predicted stunting by 11.4 percentage points. Every richer country achieves below 8% stunting. Malaysia does not.

At Malaysia's income level, stunting should be around 10%. It is 21%. Inside Malaysia, Sabah reaches 35% — a rate typical of Sub-Saharan Africa. The rot starts long before a child sees a hospital: it starts with whether their first years are spent in a home with clean water, safe food, and adequate nutrition. "Infrastructure shapes your first years. What does a lifetime of inequality do to your body by 40, 50, 60?"

PEKA B40 — Malaysia's free health screening programme for low-income households — maps the geography of NCD vulnerability. Kelantan, Kedah, Terengganu: the states with the highest poverty rates also have the most residents enrolled. More than half of eligible Kelantanese have been screened. In KL, barely one in six. The programme is reaching where the burden is greatest — but even the best-covered state has yet to reach half its at-risk population. "All of this converges in one final number: how many Malaysians die before they should. And it has a very specific address."
